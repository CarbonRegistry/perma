How can I make a Perma link?
Perma links can be created on the front page. All it takes is the URL of the page you would like to archive. We'll grab it and give you a Perma link that you can always use.
How can I keep track of my Perma links?
The last few Perma links that you created are shown at the bottom of the front page, as long as you have cookies enabled in your browser. If you register for an account, you can also see all your links in the management console.
Will you preserve my Perma link forever?
When a link is first created, it is "unvested." Unvested may be deleted from time to time, and are subject to page views to prevent abuse. A member of any editor may vest that the link is actually being used in a scholarly publication. Once a link is vested, it will never be deleted unless we receive a legitimate legal order to take it down.
Can I ever delete or edit a Perma link?
No. Perma links can never be edited once they are created, even if they are unvested.
How are links vested?
Users with editor accounts can click on the "vest" button in the top-right of any Perma page to vest it.
How do I become an editor?
Editor accounts are managed by scholarly journals that are recognized by libraries. If you are a member of a journal and would like to use Perma, please contact a library to sponsor you. You can see a list of participating libraries on the about Perma page.
How can I contact Perma?
The Perma team can be emailed at info@perma.cc. We're also on Twitter as @perma.cc.
