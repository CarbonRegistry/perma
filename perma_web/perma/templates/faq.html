General/User FAQs

What is Perma?

Perma is a service that creates citation links that will never break.

When a user creates a Perma link, Perma archives a copy of the referenced content, and generates a link to this irrevocable, unalterable hosted instance of the site. Regardless of what may happen to the original source, if the link is in a published citation, the archived version will always be available through the Perma link.

How can I make a Perma link?
Perma links can be created on the front page. All it takes is the URL of the page you would like to archive. Once you enter your URL, we'll archive the content for you and give you a Perma link that points to the archived content. If you've used a URL shortener (think bitly and the like) you'll recognize the "exchange my original URL for a new URL" pattern, just add "and archive the content at the URL" and you've fully grasped what Perma does.
How can I keep track of my Perma links?
The last few Perma links that you created are shown at the bottom of the front page, as long as you have cookies enabled in your browser. If you register for an account, you can also see all your links in the management console.
Will you preserve my Perma link forever?
When a link is first created, it is "unvested." Unvested Perma links may be deleted from time to time, and are subject to page views to prevent abuse. A member of any editor may vest that the link is actually being used in a scholarly publication. Once a link is vested, it will never be deleted unless we receive a legitimate legal order to take it down.
Can I ever delete a Perma link?
No. Perma links can never be delete once they are created.
How are links vested?
Users with editor accounts can click on the "vest" button in the top-right of any Perma page to vest it.
How do I become an editor?
Editor accounts are managed by scholarly journals that are recognized by libraries. If you are a member of a journal and would like to use Perma, please contact a library to sponsor you. You can see a list of participating libraries on the about Perma page.
How can I contact Perma?
Our Feedback tool is the best way to contact us. If you'd prefer email, reach us at info@perma.cc

Vesting Member FAQs

How do I vest a Perma link?
To vest a Perma link, simply follow the Perma URL and click the “vest” button in the upper right-hand corner.
What if an author submits an article without Perma links?
All vesting members (i.e. editors) will have the capability to both create and vest Perma links. Thus, vesting members can create Perma links for cited URLs as part of the subciting process.
How should I include the Perma link in a citation?
Each journal has the ability to formulate its own standard. However, the recommended standard is to incorporate the Perma link URL in brackets after the original URL.
How can I sign my journal up to use Perma?
Each journal has the ability to formulate its own standard. However, the recommended standard is to incorporate the Perma link URL in brackets after the original URL.

Vesting Manager FAQs

How can I sign my journal up to use Perma?
If your journal would like to become a Perma journal partner, simply email info@perma.cc, and we will connect you with a sponsoring library (traditionally the library associated with your institution). The sponsoring library will create a vesting manager account for your journal, which will enable you to create the accounts your staff will need to vest Perma links.
What are my responsibilities as a vesting manager?
The main responsibilities of a vesting manager are to serve as a liaison between the journal and its sponsoring library and to provide staff with “Vesting Member” accounts, which will allow them to vest Perma links.
How can I create accounts for my vesting members?
To create accounts for your staff members, go to the “Manage Users” tab of the dashboard, and click on “Journal Users.” Provide the requested information under “Add a new vesting member,” and click “Add vesting member.” Whether you make individual accounts for each staff member or communal accounts for those checking citations is up to your journal.

Librarian FAQs

How can my library become a partnering library?
If your library would like to become a Perma library partner, email info@perma.cc, and you will be provided with a library “Registrar” account. This account will enable your library to create “Vesting Manager” accounts for the journals your library chooses to sponsor (traditionally the journals at your institution).
What does it mean to “sponsor” a journal?
Sponsoring a journal simply means that your library, as an institution, is certifying that a particular publication is a scholarly journal of the sort that a library would subscribe to and archive.
What are my responsibilities as a Perma registrar?
As your library’s Perma registrar, your primary responsibility is provisioning administrative accounts to journals (“Vesting Manager” accounts). Journals will be able to use those accounts to give access to their staff, so you won’t have to handle account management for all the students that work on a given journal. Depending on whether your library is also hosting Perma content as an archive mirror, questions regarding your institutions notice and takedown policy may also be directed to you.
How do I make administrative accounts for journals at my school?
The process for making a Vesting Manager account is quick and simple. Once you have the email address that the journal would like associated with the account, go to the “Manage Users” section of your dashboard, click “Vesting Managers,” and provide the information requested under “Add a new vesting manager.”
