I. Creating an Account

To make PermaLinks, you will need an account. If you are a general user creating links for your own use, remember, your links will not be saved permanently until they are “vested” by a vesting user. Unvested links will be available for a period of two years.

If you are affiliated with a law library, academic law journal, or court system click here to see if your institution is a perma.cc partner and contact your institution directly for vesting privileges.

To create your account, click here or find Create An Account on the home page

Registering is easy. All we need is your email, first and last name. Be sure to read the terms of service!

If your chosen account information is available (not already taken by another user), you will see a confirmation page and receive an email at the account you specified. Please follow the instructions in the email to activate your account. If the system indicates that your email address has already been assigned to another user, it is possible that someone in your institution has already added you as a user. You can use the password reset option and your email address to claim your account.

If you are affiliated with a law library, academic law journal, or court system, you may receive an email that an account has been created for you. Follow the instructions in that email to activate your account, which will be affiliated with your particular institution including the power to vest links if assigned to you.


II. Logging into Perma

When you log in to Perma, you are taken to your Perma “Dashboard” -- a central page where you can create new PermaLinks, navigate to your existing PermaLinks, and navigate to Tools & Settings. Look for the blue Dashboard link, in the top right corner of other pages, to return here.


III. Creating a Perma Link

A Perma Link gives you access to an archived version of the content at a URL, frozen at a moment in time. You will also have an option to view the live site and compare the content. To create a Perma Link, simply input a URL into the entry box, as shown below, and press or click on the blue infinity button! The entry box is flexible and will accept multiple formats, including www and https://.

After the link is processed, a thumbnail of the original webpage will appear, now linked to a Perma Link address. The option allows you to easily copy the Perma Link for inclusion in a document, or testing on another page.

If Perma did not archive the site as you expected, you can upload your own screenshot or PDF of the desired site by clicking on the blue link at the bottom of the new link entry.

To see all URL’s that you have converted to Perma Links, click on Links in the navigation menu on the left.


IV. Organizing Links

Perma includes many useful tools for organization of links. You can file links in folders. Change the titles of your links. Even add notes that can be viewed by users who create or vest links.

You can search or sort your links using the search bar above the link list or clicking the column headings.
