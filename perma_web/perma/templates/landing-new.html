What is link rot?

When linked citations lead to broken, blank, unintentional, or even malicious pages, that’s called “link rot.”

Link rot affects you

Over 50% of cited links in Spreme Court decisions no longer point to the proper page. If you link to the Web, link rot affects you too.

Websites change, go away, and get taken down. When they do, linked citations can lead to broken, blank, unintentional, or even malicious pages. This decay is called “link rot.” Perma.cc helps scholars, journals and courts prevent link rot by creating permanent, reliable, unalterable links to the online sources cited in their work.

Link rot destroys connections to outside sources

When the citation is collected. New references to websites usually start correct.

One year later. After a year, over 20% of cited links may be dead or inacessible.

Five years later. After five years, the situation is much worse, over 50% of cited links can be afftected.

As time goes on. Link rot is rarely reversible. The longer the wait, the more likely a link will be dead.

More about your records

Making a Perma Link is as simple as copy and paste

Add the URL you want to preserve to the Create bar. Paste any URL into the Create bar and Perma.cc does the rest. Perma.cc records preserve all kinds of URLS (including PDFs) with near-perfect fidelity to the original.

Perma.cc creates your Perma Link. Just select “Create” and Perma.cc builds your new record in a matter of moments. You’ll see your record when it’s finished and have a chance to delete or annotate it.

Use the Perma Link URL in your citations. Use your Perma Link as you would the original cited URL. Perma.cc has a simple record management system to make organization and record-keeping a snap.
